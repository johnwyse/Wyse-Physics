Kinetic Energy

Kinetic Energy = (1/2)(mass)(velocity^2)
KE = (1/2)mv^2

source: https://images.slideplayer.com/26/8287330/slides/slide_2.jpg

Variable Definition Symbol Unit
Kinetic Energy Energy due to an object's motion KE Joules (J)
Mass The amount of matter in an object m kilograms (kg)
Velocity The rate at which displacement changes v meters per second (m/s)

Example Calculation

What is the kinetic energy of a 3 kg bird flying at 5 m/s

KE = ? J
m = 3 kg
v = 5 m/s

KE = (1/2)mv^2

KE = (1/2)(3)(5^2)
KE = (1/2)(3)(25)
KE = 37.5 J
